This Project explores entropy as a comprehensive and forward-looking measure of uncertainty in financial markets, positioning it as a potential complement or alternative to traditional volatility-based metrics. It focuses on option-implied distributions derived from S&P 500 index option chains. Entropy, rooted in Shannon’s information theory, measures the complexity and unpredictability of probability distributions, making it particularly suited to capture features of financial data; such as fat tails,skewness, and regime changes, which traditional Gaussian-based measures often misrepresent. By combining option pricing theory and computational simulations, this work introduces a framework for extracting, analyzing, and applying entropy-based measures in practical financial modeling and trading strategies.
